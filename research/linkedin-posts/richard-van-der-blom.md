### Summary

The 2026 report is a 200+ page, six-chapter study based on 1.3 million posts and 1,400 hours of research. It documents LinkedIn's largest algorithmic restructuring in a decade: the platform now distributes content by **topic authority** and **interest clusters**, not follower count or network size. Each chapter includes a one-page checklist. Chapter topics cover the Interest Graph shift, visibility design, format rankings, post anatomy and timing, company page strategy, and emerging areas (AI content, newsletters, groups, conversion).

Headline finding: **organic reach is down ~60% for active creators over two years**, while engagement per view has held relatively steady — consistent with LinkedIn's stated goal of "relevant reach" over virality.

### Key Takeaways

- Follower count no longer predicts reach; topic consistency and engagement quality do.
- **Saves** and **15+ word comments** outweigh likes as distribution signals.
- **Document/carousel posts** carry the highest reach multiplier (~1.39× vs. other formats).
- Posting daily reduces per-post reach by ~26–45%; **2–3 quality posts per week** outperform daily volume.
- Hashtags now **hurt** reach (0 hashtags outperform tagged posts by 5–10%); tagging 5+ people triggers spam signals if engagement is low.
- External links in post body reduce median reach by ~19%; links in comments are now suppressed (up to 80% visibility drop).
- Company page reach grew 33% while personal profile reach fell — employee advocacy matters more than ever.
- 34% of top-performing posts in 2026 are under 600 characters (up from 21% in 2024).

### Relevance to B2B SaaS Organic Strategy

This report is the foundational reference for any B2B SaaS LinkedIn program. It validates shifting resources from company-page-only posting to **founder/executive personal profiles**, justifies investing in **document-based thought leadership** (product insights, frameworks, teardown carousels), and provides data-backed guardrails against outdated tactics (hashtag stuffing, link-in-first-comment, daily posting cadences). Chapter 5 directly addresses how SaaS marketing teams should structure company pages, employee advocacy, and paid amplification in 2026.

| **Source** | [LinkedIn post](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/richardvanderblom_92-of-linkedin-posts-get-buried-the-other-activity-7454779075866423296-9Lyp) |

### Summary

A data-driven carousel identifying five behavioral and structural factors that separate the top 8% of posts from the 92% that receive negligible distribution. The post reframes success as **post-publish behavior** and **interest-cluster alignment**, not content quality alone. It served as the final pre-launch teaser for the 2026 report.

### Key Takeaways

1. **Active nurturing in the first 60 minutes** — responding to comments, adding thread insights, and reposting for different time zones signals post quality to the algorithm.
2. **50+ likes from outside your network** — but only from your interest cluster, not random engagement.
3. **Above 1.5× median saves and reposts** — saves signal "I want to be associated with this content."
4. **Consistent track record → "Suggested" placement** — sustained value in your cluster earns free distribution to non-followers (~9% of feed is suggested posts).
5. **No hashtags, no tags** — the negative impact of both now outweighs any discovery benefit.

Additional data surfaced in community summaries: replying to comments within 30 minutes in the first 4 hours yields **+64% more comments and 2.3× more views**; 97.7% of posts get zero saves, but a single save adds ~65% more impressions.

### Relevance to B2B SaaS Organic Strategy

B2B SaaS teams often treat LinkedIn as "publish and move on." This post makes the case for **post-publish workflows** — assigning someone to nurture threads in the first hour, scheduling reposts for US/EU time zones, and designing content worth saving (checklists, ROI calculators, competitive frameworks). For lean SaaS marketing teams, the 60-minute nurture window is a concrete operational SOP, not a vague "engage more" recommendation.

## Item 3: "360Brew Changed Everything" — Post-Update Data Analysis

| Field | Details |
|---|---|
| **Type** | LinkedIn text post (algorithm update breakdown) |
| **Date** | February 2026 |
| **Source** | [LinkedIn post](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/richardvanderblom_ready-for-another-algorithm-update-because-activity-7417834264173551617-wtjr) |

### Summary

Following LinkedIn's rollout of **360Brew** (its proprietary LLM architecture replacing embedding-based retrieval), Richard analyzed post-update data from 2M+ posts over 18 months. The post outlines five measurable shifts in how content is classified, ranked, and distributed — moving from hackable engagement metrics to semantic depth and entity relationships.

### Key Takeaways

1. **Network composition > follower count** — posts from accounts you regularly engage with get 3.2× more visibility than random viral content.
2. **Dwell time beats likes 2.8×** — posts with strong read time outperform high-like, low-dwell content.
3. **WHO posts matters** — profile, skills, and content history infer expertise; on-topic posts get up to **47% more distribution**; off-topic posts get buried.
4. **Comment quality over vanity** — comments of 15+ words boost reach 2.5× more than short replies; "Great post! 🔥" does not.
5. **AI-generated and generic content gets buried** — AI comments receive 7× less engagement; original posts with distinctive perspectives see 34% higher reach.

Additional guidance from the same research thread: the **first 60 tokens** (2–3 lines) carry disproportionate LLM weight — front-load authority with specific metrics and ICP keywords. Profile bios function as **"LLM SEO"** — the algorithm builds a semantic profile to match you with audiences.

### Relevance to B2B SaaS Organic Strategy

360Brew means LinkedIn reads content semantically, not just socially. For B2B SaaS, this reinforces **narrow positioning**: a VP of Sales posting about "pipeline velocity for mid-market SaaS" will outperform the same person posting about leadership quotes, hiring, and product updates interchangeably. SaaS founders should audit profiles and recent posts with an AI tool ("What are my two main topics?") and align 80% of content to 1–3 related pillars. This directly supports demand-gen strategies that depend on reaching specific buyer personas, not broad audiences.

## Content Strategy Insights

### The Interest Graph reset changes the game for B2B SaaS

LinkedIn no longer rewards large networks or posting volume. It rewards **semantic clarity**: a profile, content history, and engagement pattern that consistently signal one expertise domain. For B2B SaaS, this means the "generalist founder" posting about culture, fundraising, product, and industry news is actively working against the algorithm. Narrow positioning is now a distribution strategy, not just a branding preference.

### Engagement hierarchy has inverted

The 2026 engagement stack (highest to lowest value): **saves → 15+ word comments → reposts → dwell time → likes → reactions**. B2B SaaS marketers measuring success by impressions or likes are optimizing for deprecated signals. Content should be designed to be **saved and referenced** — frameworks, benchmarks, decision guides — not just consumed and scrolled past.

### Format is a strategic choice, not a creative preference

Documents/carousels lead; video has declined; text-only works only for elite copywriters. B2B SaaS teams with design resources should prioritize carousel production pipelines. Teams without design capacity should invest in writing quality or simple document templates rather than defaulting to video because it "feels modern."

### Post-publish behavior is half the strategy

Publishing is the beginning, not the end. The first 60 minutes determine whether a post enters suggested distribution. B2B SaaS teams need explicit post-publish SOPs: who nurtures the thread, when to repost for time zones, and how to respond to AI-generated comments without rewarding them.

### Third-party tool dependency is a risk

Shield, UseAware, and automation tools are being shut down. Richard's **bookmark-search feed** technique (free, platform-native) is the sustainable alternative for curated engagement lists. SaaS teams should audit tool compliance before building workflows around non-compliant automation.

## Practical Lessons for B2B SaaS Marketers

### 1. Audit your topic fingerprint before your content calendar

Copy your last 20 posts into Claude or ChatGPT. Ask: "What are my two main topics?" If the answer doesn't match your ICP and product category, pause publishing and realign. Revamp your headline, About section, and featured content to match. Richard saw measurable reach improvement within three weeks of his own profile overhaul.

### 2. Shift from daily posting to 2–3 high-intensity posts per week

Reduce volume. Increase depth. Each post should be worth saving. Batch-create document carousels from existing assets: customer case studies, product comparison decks, implementation guides, and quarterly benchmark reports.

### 3. Build a document/carousel production pipeline

Assign format multipliers from the report: documents (~1.39× reach) > mixed formats > text-only. Repurpose one research asset (internal data, customer survey, product release) into a 6–10 slide carousel per week. This is the highest-ROI format for B2B SaaS thought leadership in 2026.

### 4. Implement a 60-minute post-publish SOP

After every post: respond to all comments within 30 minutes, add value in the thread, repost 4–6 hours later for secondary time zones, and never edit in the first hour. Assign this to the post author or a dedicated community manager — not an intern running automation.

### 5. Replace engagement-bait lead gen with save-worthy content

Retire "comment SEND for the playbook" CTAs. LinkedIn's AI moderation is actively removing these posts. Instead, deliver the framework inline (carousel) or via a LinkedIn newsletter subscription. Saves and substantive comments generate better distribution *and* higher-quality leads.

### 6. Spend 30–50% of LinkedIn time in comments, not composing posts

Use bookmark-search feeds to curate daily engagement lists: 10–12 ICP peers, 5–10 prospects, 5–10 industry creators. Leave 15+ word comments that demonstrate expertise. Avoid commenting off-topic — it dilutes your interest cluster.

### 7. Launch or revive a LinkedIn newsletter

Newsletter posts outperform standalone posts on reach, engagement, and conversion. For B2B SaaS, repurpose your existing email newsletter or product update as a LinkedIn newsletter edition. This creates a compounding subscriber asset independent of the algorithm.

### 8. Treat company pages as amplification, not the primary channel

Company page reach grew 33% while personal profiles declined — but personal profiles still drive trust and pipeline. Structure your program as **founder/executive-led content** with company page reshares and employee advocacy, not the reverse.

### 9. Measure what matters in 2026

Track saves, comment word count, reposts, profile visits from target accounts, and suggested-post appearances — not raw impressions or follower growth. Align weekly reviews to these metrics.

For B2B SaaS marketing teams making budget decisions on content headcount, tools, and cadence, the report pays for itself in avoided tactical mistakes (daily posting, hashtag use, link-in-comment, engagement pods).
